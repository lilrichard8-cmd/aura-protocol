# AURA Litepaper

**The Creator Economy Infrastructure**
*2-minute read for the Colosseum jury · Built by Søren & Iris*

---

## Why

The creator economy has been an act of borrowing.
Creators borrow audiences from platforms. Fans borrow ownership from no one.
Every algorithm change resets a million careers overnight.

Web3 was supposed to fix this. Instead it gave us P2E ponzis and rug-pulls.

---

## AURA is infrastructure, not a platform

We are not building "a Web3 social network."

We are building the **substrate** for the creator economy — the on-chain primitives (identity, tokens, content storage, settlement, reputation) that any frontend can plug into.

**Our design philosophy is one sentence:**
> *A creator can issue a Creator Coin, a fan can tip them, a curator can earn for discovery — and none of them needs to know what a wallet, gas fee, or seed phrase is.*

If the user has to learn Web3, we failed.

This is what "infrastructure" means here: the protocol does the hard things (signing, settlement, storage, deflationary economics) so that humans can do the human things — make, watch, share, support.

---

## Open frontend ecosystem — the core, not the footnote

The protocol is shared substrate. **Frontends compete on top of it.**

- **Mainstream** — official, free, the reference implementation
- **AfterDark** — adult-content frontend, separate compliance scope
- **Anyone else** — register permissionlessly, run any policy you want (KYC, regional gateway, content rules) without forking the protocol

Frontends share **95% of ad revenue and protocol fees**. The protocol takes 5%.
Users carry one identity — Creator Coins, followers, reputation, transaction history — across **every** frontend. Switching takes one click. Nothing is rebuilt.

This is how we resolve the compliance dilemma that breaks every Web3 social attempt:
> **The protocol stays open. The frontends stay accountable.**

A creator in Tokyo, a frontend in Singapore, a fan in Lagos — same network, different doorways, no central permission.

## Five pillars

**1. Creator Coin — every creator is a micro-economy**
Cross 100 followers, mint a free 10,000-supply Creator Coin. 2,000 unlock immediately, 8,000 vest over 10 months gated by activity. Holders unlock perks — *holding-type* (access while you hold) or *consumable-type* (burn to redeem).
Utility, not security. No royalties. Just access.

**2. Curation Mining — discovery is labor**
Spend 1 ORA to curate. If the content grows, you earn from the curation pool. Multipliers reward early discovery (up to 5×) and unknown talent (5× for sub-100-follower creators). A first curator on hidden talent can earn **25× base reward**.
Rewards taste over reach.

**3. Portable social graph**
Followers, reputation (SBT), Creator Coins, content (Arweave) — all on-chain. If we disappear tomorrow, every creator walks away with their full graph intact.

**4. ORA — deflationary by design**
1.1B initial. Annual emission 5% → 4% → 3% → permanent 2% floor. Hard-coded.
**One unified 5% protocol fee on every ORA transfer**: 2% burn / 2% staking / 0.5% gas / 0.5% ops.
**Dual burn**: 5% scarcity burn on every reward distribution + 2% on every transfer. The economy contracts as it is used.

**5. Permanent storage**
Every post pinned to Arweave. The frontend can disappear. The work cannot.

## The 5% rule

Every time ORA moves, the protocol takes **5% — that's it**.

- **Fan tips creator** → 95% to creator, 5% to protocol
- **Advertiser pays a frontend** → 95% to the frontend, 5% to protocol
- **Pay with the creator's own Creator Coin** → 100% to creator (5% was already collected when the coin was minted)

No tiered rates. No hidden fees. No exceptions.

## The double doorway — infrastructure in action

The protocol does not care how you got in.

- **Web2 users** sign in with email or phone. A non-custodial MPC wallet is created behind the scenes. They never see a seed phrase. They never pay gas.
- **Web3 users** click *Connect Wallet*. Their existing Phantom is their identity.

Same protocol. Same features. Two doorways.

This is the infrastructure philosophy made concrete: **Web3 should serve users — not the other way around.**
